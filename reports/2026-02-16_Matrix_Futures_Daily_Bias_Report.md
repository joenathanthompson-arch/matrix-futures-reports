## Key Market Drivers

The following are the top three macroeconomic factors influencing today's market bias:

1.  **Broad-Based US Dollar Weakness:** The US Dollar Index (DXY) has declined significantly, falling 2.42% over the past month and 9.48% over the past year. This weakness provides a tailwind for assets priced in dollars, such as commodities and foreign currencies.
2.  **Dovish Federal Reserve Stance:** The Federal Reserve is maintaining a dovish hold on interest rates, with market participants pricing in a small but notable probability of a near-term rate cut. This accommodative stance is generally supportive of risk assets like equities.
3.  **Central Bank Policy Divergence:** While the Fed remains dovish, other major central banks are pursuing different paths. The Reserve Bank of Australia recently hiked rates, and the Bank of Japan has adopted a hawkish tone, signaling potential future tightening. This divergence is creating significant opportunities in FX markets.
