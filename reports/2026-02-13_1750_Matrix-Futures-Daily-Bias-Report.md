**ES (S&P 500):** The S&P 500 shows a bullish bias (+4). The primary drivers are very tight credit spreads, a falling VIX, and a weaker USD, creating a supportive environment for large-cap equities. The neutral Fed stance and stable growth outlook provide a solid foundation. Recommended approach: IB_BREAKOUT (7/10).

**NQ (Nasdaq 100):** The Nasdaq 100 has a slight bullish bias (+2). It benefits from a weaker USD and a strong semiconductor sector (SOX). However, a recent rise in the MOVE index, indicating higher interest rate volatility, is a headwind for long-duration tech stocks, reducing the overall conviction. Recommended approach: MEAN_REVERSION (6/10).

**YM (Dow Jones):** The Dow Jones shows a bullish bias (+4), benefiting from tight credit spreads and a steepening 2s10s yield curve, which is often positive for industrial and financial sectors. The stable growth narrative further supports the outlook for value-oriented stocks. Recommended approach: IB_BREAKOUT (7/10).

**RTY (Russell 2000):** The Russell 2000 exhibits a strong bullish bias (+5). Small-cap stocks are highly sensitive to credit conditions, and the extremely tight high-yield spreads (2.92%) provide a powerful tailwind. A steepening yield curve and improving risk appetite further bolster the case for domestic small caps. Recommended approach: IB_BREAKOUT (7/10).

**M6E (Euro):** The Euro shows a slight bullish bias (+2) against the US Dollar. This is almost entirely a reflection of broad USD weakness, as Eurozone-specific factors like ECB stance and growth are currently neutral. Recommended approach: RANGE (5/10).

**6A (Australian Dollar):** The Australian Dollar has a slight bullish bias (+2). The primary driver is strong risk sentiment (falling VIX) and a weaker USD. This is partially offset by falling copper prices, a key Australian export, which tempers the bullish outlook. Recommended approach: RANGE (6/10).

**6J (Japanese Yen):** The Japanese Yen shows a slight bullish bias (+2). The risk-off reading from the VIX (>20) provides safe-haven demand for the Yen. This is coupled with broad USD weakness, making the JPY attractive. Note that a bullish 6J view implies a bearish outlook for the USD/JPY pair. Recommended approach: MEAN_REVERSION (6/10).

## Key Macro Themes
- **Accommodative Financial Conditions:** Extremely tight credit spreads are the dominant theme, signaling easy financial conditions and strong investor appetite for risk, which is highly supportive for equities.
- **Broad USD Weakness:** The US Dollar is weakening against its major trading partners, providing a tailwind for commodities and foreign currencies.
- **Conflicting Volatility Signals:** While the VIX remains above the 20 threshold for risk-off, its downward trajectory suggests improving risk appetite. However, the MOVE index (bond volatility) is rising, creating a headwind for duration-sensitive assets like tech stocks.
