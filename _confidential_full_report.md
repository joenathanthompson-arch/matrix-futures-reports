## 2. Instrument Bias Breakdown

### 2.1. Commodities

**GC (Gold): STRONG_BULLISH (+5)**
Gold benefits from a confluence of bullish factors. The dovish Fed stance and a weak dollar provide a strong tailwind. Slowing growth, rising geopolitical risk, and consistent ETF inflows further bolster the bullish case. The only neutral factor is the flat real yield environment.

**SI (Silver): STRONG_BULLISH (+5)**
Silver's bias mirrors Gold's, with the added benefit of a rising copper price, indicating strong industrial demand. The combination of monetary tailwinds and industrial strength creates a powerful bullish signal.

**CL (WTI Crude): STRONG_BULLISH (+7)**
Crude oil exhibits the highest conviction score today. Escalating geopolitical risk in the Middle East provides a significant supply-side catalyst. This is compounded by a confirmed inventory draw and a weak US Dollar, creating a potent bullish cocktail.

### 2.2. Equity Indices

**ES (S&P 500): SLIGHT_BULLISH (+1)**
The S&P 500 shows a slight bullish bias. A dovish Fed and narrowing credit spreads are supportive. However, a slowing growth narrative and a recent uptick in the VIX act as headwinds, muting the overall bullishness.

**NQ (NASDAQ 100): SLIGHT_BULLISH (+1)**
The Nasdaq 100 shares a similar profile to the S&P 500. The continued strength in the semiconductor sector (SOX) is a positive driver. However, rising interest rate volatility (MOVE index) creates uncertainty for long-duration tech stocks.

**YM (Dow Jones): SLIGHT_BULLISH (+2)**
The Dow Jones has a slightly stronger bullish bias than its counterparts. The steepening 2s10s yield curve is a positive signal for the more cyclically-oriented companies in the index. The slowing growth narrative remains the primary concern.

**RTY (Russell 2000): BULLISH (+3)**
The Russell 2000 shows the most bullish sentiment among the indices. Small-cap stocks are highly sensitive to credit conditions, and the significant narrowing of high-yield spreads provides a strong boost. A steepening yield curve further supports the case for an economic re-acceleration that would benefit smaller companies.

### 2.3. Foreign Exchange

**M6E (Micro Euro FX): SLIGHT_BULLISH (+2)**
The Euro's bullish bias is almost entirely a function of US Dollar weakness. With the ECB on a neutral hold and Eurozone growth stable, there are few independent drivers for Euro strength. The primary signal is the broad-based dollar decline.

**6A (Australian Dollar): BULLISH (+3)**
The Australian Dollar benefits from multiple tailwinds. The rising price of copper, a key export, is a significant positive. This is combined with broad US Dollar weakness. The RBA's hawkish stance, while a headwind for the domestic economy, provides a supportive interest rate differential.

**6J (Japanese Yen): STRONG_BULLISH (+6)**
The Japanese Yen shows a strong bullish signal. The primary driver is the Bank of Japan's hawkish tilt and the expectation of further policy tightening. This is amplified by a narrowing interest rate differential with the US and the overall weakness of the US Dollar.
