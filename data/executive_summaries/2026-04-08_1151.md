### SI (Silver): +3 STRONG_BULLISH (6/10)
**Approach:** TREND_FOLLOW | **Mode:** SWING | **Hold:** 2-5 days
Silver is supported by the same **weak-dollar** and **defensive-macro** tailwinds helping gold, but it also benefits from a distinctly stronger industrial overlay because **copper is rising sharply**.[2] [5] [10] [12] That combination makes silver’s profile more balanced than pure safe-haven metals, though the softer U.S. growth narrative remains a caveat for follow-through if cyclical sentiment fades.[6]

### CL (Crude Oil): +3 STRONG_BULLISH (7/10)
**Approach:** TREND_FOLLOW | **Mode:** SWING | **Hold:** 2-5 days
Crude remains strongly supported by the verified **Strait of Hormuz disruption**, with EIA-linked reporting describing material supply impairment, outages that rose from **7.5 million bpd in March** toward **9.1 million bpd in April**, and a projected global inventory draw in Q2.[8] The main offset is the latest **5.5 million barrel U.S. crude inventory build**, which tempers the outright bullishness of the supply story.[11] Even so, the broader balance of evidence still favors oil strength while disruption risk remains unresolved.

## Key Macro Themes

1. **Dollar weakness is the main cross-asset transmission channel.** DXY fell to **98.726**, supporting metals, crude, and non-USD FX at the same time.[2]
2. **Volatility is easing, but not fully benign.** VIX fell sharply to **20.27** and MOVE also declined, which is supportive for equities and duration-sensitive trades, yet the absolute VIX level still sits in the methodology’s risk-off bucket.[1] [3]
3. **Growth is diverging across regions.** U.S. GDPNow is only **1.3%**, euro-area PMI slowed materially, while China’s official manufacturing PMI improved back into expansion at **50.4**.[6] [7] [16]
4. **Oil-market stress remains structurally important.** The inventory build in the latest weekly U.S. report does not outweigh the broader Hormuz-related supply disruption, so crude and geopolitical-risk factors remain positive.[8] [11]
