## Key Macro Themes

**USD Weakness Broadens:** The US Dollar's decline continues to accelerate, falling 0.84% over the past week and 2.26% over the month. This broad-based weakness is providing a tailwind across multiple asset classes, particularly commodities and foreign currencies. With the Fed on hold and no imminent catalyst for USD strength, this trend appears likely to persist near-term.

**Credit Conditions Remain Supportive:** High yield credit spreads have narrowed to 2.92%, signaling healthy risk appetite and strong access to capital markets. This is particularly bullish for small caps (RTY) and cyclical sectors, which rely heavily on credit availability. Combined with a steepening yield curve (2s10s at 0.64%), the credit environment favors risk-taking in equity markets.

**Policy Divergence Drives FX:** The most compelling macro story is the policy divergence between the Bank of Japan (hawkish/tightening) and the Federal Reserve (neutral hold). This divergence is creating a strong directional bias in JPY, offering the highest conviction trade in the FX space. Meanwhile, other central banks (ECB, RBA) remain in neutral stances, limiting conviction in EUR and AUD.
