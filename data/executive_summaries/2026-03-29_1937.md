## Key Macro Themes

The dominant theme this week is the collision between rising US real yields and geopolitical risk from the US-Israel war against Iran. Real yields have risen approximately 20bps over the past two weeks to 2.08%, creating a hostile environment for gold, equities, and risk assets simultaneously. The MOVE index has surged 71% in one month, reflecting genuine uncertainty about the rate path as the Iran war introduces inflationary pressures through oil prices.

The second major theme is central bank divergence. The BoJ is explicitly hawkish and hiking (0.75%, with more expected), the ECB has shifted to a hawkish hold with 60bps of hikes priced for 2026, and the RBA has delivered two consecutive hikes to 4.10% — all while the Fed sits at 3.50-3.75% with a 97.9% probability of no change at the April 30 meeting. This creates a clear structural tailwind for JPY and EUR against USD, even as the DXY holds above 100 on safe-haven demand.

The third theme is the oil market paradox: five consecutive weeks of massive inventory builds (+6.9M barrels in the latest week) signal fundamental oversupply, yet geopolitical risk from the Iran war and Hormuz supply fears provide a persistent floor. This creates a difficult trading environment for CL with high volatility and conflicting fundamental signals.
