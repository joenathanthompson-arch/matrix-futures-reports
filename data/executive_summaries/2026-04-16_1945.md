# Matrix Futures Daily Bias Report

## Data Quality Notes

The report is based on verified checks across official central-bank releases, FRED series, exchange quote pages, the EIA weekly petroleum summary, and World Gold Council research. **DFII10** remained one trading day behind spot market pricing on FRED, so it was treated as an official but lagged source. CME FedWatch did not expose its probability table cleanly during collection, so Federal Reserve calendar materials were used as the official Fed-stance fallback. The public ETF data page at the World Gold Council also gated detailed charts behind sign-in, so the accessible March 2026 research note was used to verify the gold-ETF flow direction.

## Asset Class Overview

- **Commodities:** **BEARISH** - Precious metals remain pressured by restrictive U.S. policy expectations and record March gold-ETF outflows, while crude retains a separate bullish inventory-and-geopolitics backdrop.
- **Indices:** **BEARISH** - Broad equity beta remains restrained by a still-restrictive Fed backdrop, a modestly firmer intraday dollar tone, and credit conditions that are no longer improving.
- **FX:** **BEARISH** - The broader complex still tilts toward USD resilience overall, although AUD remains relatively stronger than the euro and yen thanks to the RBA-China mix.

## Symbol Analysis

**GC (Gold):** Gold carries a **SLIGHT_BEARISH (-2)** bias because restrictive Fed expectations, record March ETF outflows, and only flat real-yield relief continue to offset the supportive geopolitical backdrop. The broader dollar trend has softened over multiple sessions, but the intraday tone is still not cleanly supportive enough to reverse the signal. Recommended approach: **SELL_RALLIES (6/10)**.

**SI (Silver):** Silver shows a **BEARISH (-4)** bias because precious-metals fund-flow pressure remains negative and the macro backdrop still leans against clean upside follow-through. Silver also lacks a decisive growth acceleration tailwind strong enough to neutralize the broader headwinds facing the metals complex. Recommended approach: **TREND_SHORT (7/10)**.

**CL (Crude):** WTI crude is **BULLISH (+3)** after the official EIA summary confirmed a **0.9 million barrel inventory draw** for the latest reported week, while geopolitical tensions continue to keep supply-risk premia elevated. The dollar is a headwind, but the inventory and conflict backdrop remain more important for the current crude setup. Recommended approach: **BUY_PULLBACKS (7/10)**.

**ES (S&P 500):** The S&P 500 bias is **SLIGHT_BEARISH (-2)** because restrictive Fed expectations and a firmer intraday dollar tone offset the constructive decline in VIX. High-yield spreads are no longer providing clear incremental support, so the index backdrop remains cautious rather than outright risk-on. Recommended approach: **SELL_RALLIES (5/10)**.

**NQ (Nasdaq):** Nasdaq is **NEUTRAL (0)** because rising semiconductor leadership and a clearly falling MOVE index offset the macro drag from Fed restrictiveness and the dollar. That leaves the contract in a balanced tactical regime rather than a high-conviction directional trend. Recommended approach: **RANGE_SCALP (5/10)**.

**YM (Dow):** The Dow carries a **SLIGHT_BEARISH (-2)** bias as modest curve steepening is not enough to fully offset the broader restrictive-policy backdrop and steadier dollar tone. The macro mix still favors defensive positioning over aggressive long exposure. Recommended approach: **SELL_RALLIES (5/10)**.

**RTY (Russell 2000):** Russell 2000 is **SLIGHT_BEARISH (-3)** because smaller-cap balance sheets remain more sensitive to tight financial conditions, while high-yield spreads are no longer clearly tightening. Even with a modestly steeper curve, the funding backdrop is not supportive enough to lift the signal above bearish. Recommended approach: **TREND_SHORT (6/10)**.

**M6E (Euro):** The euro future is **SLIGHT_BEARISH (-3)** because the ECB only delivered a mildly hawkish hold, euro-area growth momentum remains soft, and the dollar retains enough near-term firmness to cap upside. The medium-horizon DXY trend is softer than the intraday move suggests, but not enough to reverse the contract's current bias. Recommended approach: **SELL_RALLIES (6/10)**.

**6A (Australian Dollar):** The Australian dollar future is **SLIGHT_BULLISH (+2)** as the RBA's March hike and China's stronger March PMI continue to cushion the contract against broader USD resilience. AUD remains the most constructive FX contract in the basket, although conviction stays moderate rather than strong. Recommended approach: **BUY_PULLBACKS (6/10)**.

**6J (Japanese Yen):** The yen future is **SLIGHT_BEARISH (-2)** because the BoJ remains in a hold posture, the U.S.-Japan rate gap is still wide, and VIX remains in a balanced range rather than a true risk-off regime. That prevents the yen from receiving enough safe-haven demand to turn constructive. Recommended approach: **SELL_RALLIES (6/10)**.

## Key Macro Themes

- The Fed remains on hold, the ECB and BoJ are still in hold regimes, and the RBA remains the clearest hawkish outlier in the policy mix.
- Real yields are broadly flat on the latest official FRED reading, while the dollar is firmer intraday even though its broader multi-session trend has softened.
- Oil is still benefiting from the combination of a verified inventory draw and persistent geopolitical supply concerns.
