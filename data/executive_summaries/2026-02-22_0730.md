## Key Macro Themes

1.  **Falling Real Yields Driving the Risk-On Regime:** The 10-year TIPS yield has declined to 1.79% (FRED DFII10, February 19), down from approximately 2.0% in late 2025. This compression is the single most powerful macro driver, simultaneously bullish for gold, silver, US equities (especially NQ), and risk assets broadly. The Fed's neutral hold means this compression is driven by moderating growth expectations rather than a panic pivot, making it a more durable signal.
2.  **Central Bank Divergence Creating FX Opportunities:** The RBA's hawkish hike to 3.85% and the BoJ's normalization trajectory toward 1.00% contrast sharply with the Fed's neutral hold. This divergence is creating clear, sustained trends in AUD and JPY, both of which are strengthening against the USD. This theme is expected to persist through H1 2026.
3.  **Commodity Supercycle Momentum:** Gold (up 74% year-over-year), silver (up 41% year-to-date), and copper (up 28.8% year-to-date) are all showing significant upward momentum, driven by a weak dollar, specific supply/demand imbalances, and strong investor inflows. The geopolitical risk premium in oil adds another layer to the commodity bull case.
4.  **US-Iran Geopolitical Risk Elevating Oil:** Trump has set a deadline for an Iranian nuclear deal, with the possibility of US military action. This is keeping a significant risk premium embedded in oil prices, supporting WTI near 6-month highs. Any escalation could lead to a rapid price spike.
5.  **Structural USD Weakness:** The DXY at ~97.80 represents a significant decline from the 107+ highs, and the index remains in a downtrend. This broad-based dollar weakness is a tailwind for almost every other asset class, from commodities to foreign currencies and US equities.
