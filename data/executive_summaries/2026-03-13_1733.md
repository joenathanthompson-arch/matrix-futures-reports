1. **Falling Real Yields as the Dominant Driver:** The 10-year TIPS yield (DFII10) has fallen to 1.89%, down more than 5bps from the ~2.0% range. This is the single most important factor in today's report, contributing +4 weighted points to both GC and ES, and +2 to NQ. The falling real yield environment is simultaneously bullish for gold, equities, and long-duration assets — creating an unusual alignment where both safe havens and risk assets are scoring bullish.

2. **Geopolitical Risk Premium Persists:** The US-Iran conflict continues to drive elevated oil prices (WTI at $90.77) and a risk-off VIX (27.19). This creates a bifurcated market: safe havens (gold, JPY) and energy (crude) benefit from the geopolitical premium, while risk currencies (AUD) and credit-sensitive assets (RTY) suffer. The Iran situation remains the key wildcard — any escalation or de-escalation will have outsized market impact.

3. **Pre-FOMC Paralysis Approaching:** With the Federal Reserve meeting in 5 days (March 18-19), the RBA meeting on March 18, the BoJ meeting on March 18-19, and the ECB meeting on March 19, the next week represents the most concentrated central bank event risk of 2026. This "central bank week" is suppressing conviction across all instruments and is the primary reason confidence scores are capped at 6-7/10 rather than 8-9/10.
