## Key Macro Themes

The dominant macro theme entering April 5 is the intersection of falling real yields and geopolitical risk premium. The 10-year TIPS yield at 1.97% (down ~13bps from recent highs) is compressing the equity risk premium and supporting gold simultaneously, creating an unusual environment where both risk assets and safe havens are scoring bullish. This divergence is typically resolved by a catalyst — either geopolitical de-escalation (which would hurt gold and JPY but support equities) or credit stress escalation (which would hurt equities but support gold and JPY).

The second theme is central bank divergence. The BoJ and ECB are both pivoting hawkish while the Fed holds neutral, creating FX rebalancing flows away from the USD. This is supportive for M6E and 6J, and partially supportive for 6A (offset by risk-off sentiment).

The third theme is the oil market paradox: large US crude inventory builds (+5.45M bbls) coexist with WTI above $100/barrel due to the Strait of Hormuz disruption. This tension will resolve when either the geopolitical situation de-escalates or US production/inventory data overwhelms the geopolitical premium.
