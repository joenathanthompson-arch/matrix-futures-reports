## Key Macro Themes

The dominant macro theme this week is the **Iran war and Strait of Hormuz crisis**, which has created a bifurcated market: energy markets are in a supply-shock bull run while risk assets face a broad-based selloff. The effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz threatens approximately 20% of global oil supply, driving WTI to $90.90/barrel (+36% in one week) and creating a stagflation risk that is particularly damaging for central bank credibility.

**Rising real yields** (10Y TIPS at 1.82%, up +10bps from Feb 27) are the second major theme, creating a direct headwind for equities (especially tech/NQ), gold, and silver. The MOVE index surging +27% in 5 days reflects genuine uncertainty about the Fed's path if energy-driven inflation re-accelerates.

**USD safe-haven demand** is the third theme, with DXY near 99 (+1% on the week). This creates a headwind for commodities priced in USD (partially offsetting the oil supply shock for CL), risk-sensitive currencies (AUD), and precious metals.
