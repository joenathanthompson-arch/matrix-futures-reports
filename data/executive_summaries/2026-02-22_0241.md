## Key Macro Themes

**The Real Yield Compression Trade:** The 10Y TIPS yield has fallen to 1.79% (FRED DFII10, Feb 19) from approximately 2.0% in late 2025, a decline of more than 20 basis points. This is the single most powerful macro driver in the current environment, simultaneously bullish for gold, silver, US equities (especially tech/NQ), and risk assets broadly. The Fed's neutral hold posture means this compression is driven by growth expectations moderating rather than a panic pivot, making it a more durable signal.

**USD Structural Weakness:** The DXY at 97.789 represents an 8.15% decline over the past year, and the index is in a confirmed 2026 downtrend (-1.01% over the past month, -0.45% YTD). This provides a broad tailwind for commodities (priced in USD), non-USD FX pairs, and gold. The combination of the Fed on hold while the BOJ and RBA hike is accelerating this divergence and is expected to persist through H1 2026.

**BOJ Normalization as the Structural JPY Theme:** The Bank of Japan's multi-year journey from ultra-loose policy toward normalization is the most significant structural FX theme. With the BOJ expected to reach 1.00% by mid-2026 and the Fed holding or cutting, the USD/JPY carry trade is unwinding, driving sustained JPY strength. Japan's January inflation data (core CPI below 2% for the first time in two years) introduces some timing uncertainty but does not alter the direction of the BOJ's hiking cycle.
