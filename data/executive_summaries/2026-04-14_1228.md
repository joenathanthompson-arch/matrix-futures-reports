**RTY (Russell 2000):** Russell 2000 is **slight bullish** at **+2 (SLIGHT_BULLISH)**. Domestic-growth softness and a weaker dollar provide support, but the instrument's higher sensitivity to credit means wider HY spreads still suppress conviction more than for the larger-cap indices [1] [3] [8]. This looks tradable on breakout continuation, though not yet a broad-based small-cap regime shift. Recommended approach: **IB_BREAKOUT (5/10)**.

**6E (Euro):** The euro future is **slight bullish** at **+1 (SLIGHT_BULLISH)**. The dominant support comes from broad USD weakness, while the ECB's March decision to leave rates unchanged with still-restrictive anti-inflation language prevents the euro from losing carry support altogether [1] [15]. The offset is weaker euro-area growth momentum, so this remains a mild rather than forceful long bias. Recommended approach: **IB_BREAKOUT (5/10)**.

**6A (Australian Dollar):** AUD is the strongest FX long at **+5 (STRONG_BULLISH)**. Balanced global risk mood prevents an even larger score, but the cyclical impulse is clearly favorable. Recommended approach: **TREND_FOLLOW (7/10)**.

**6J (Japanese Yen):** Yen futures are **bullish** at **+3 (BULLISH)**.
