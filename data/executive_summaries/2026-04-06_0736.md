## Key Macro Themes

**Theme 1: The Iran War Geopolitical Premium.** US-Israeli strikes on Iran (day 38) with Trump threatening Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz have created a persistent geopolitical risk premium across safe havens (gold, JPY) and energy (crude oil). WTI at $101.26 reflects both supply disruption risk and a demand-destruction offset from slowing US growth (GDPNow 1.6%). The premium is self-sustaining as long as the conflict continues, but any ceasefire headline would be a sharp reversal catalyst across all geopolitically-driven positions.

**Theme 2: Non-USD Central Bank Hawkish Divergence.** The ECB, BoJ, and RBA are all in hawkish mode relative to the Fed's neutral hold at 3.50-3.75%. The ECB is expected to hike at April 29 (Villeroy confirmed next move is "very likely" a rise), the BoJ has a 70% probability of hiking at April 27, and the RBA hiked to 4.10% on March 17 with a 4.85% peak expected. This creates a structural USD headwind and is the dominant FX theme for the next 3-4 weeks. M6E and 6J are the primary beneficiaries; 6A benefits but is constrained by risk-off sentiment.

**Theme 3: Rate Volatility Easing Supports Risk Assets.** The MOVE index has fallen -26.61% over five days and -6.41% on April 3, signaling that bond market volatility is easing despite the elevated VIX (24.99). This disconnect — high equity volatility but falling rate volatility — suggests that the current risk-off episode is geopolitically driven rather than fundamentally driven by credit stress. When MOVE falls while VIX is elevated, equities and long-duration assets (NQ) historically recover once the geopolitical catalyst resolves. This supports the bullish NQ and YM bias.
