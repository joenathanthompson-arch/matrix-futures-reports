## Key Macro Themes

- **Iran War Geopolitical Shock:** US-Israel strikes on Iran have triggered a classic risk-off rotation — VIX spiked to 23.76, oil surged (WTI >$80, Brent ~$84), safe havens (JPY, gold partially) bid, equities sold off. The Strait of Hormuz supply disruption threat is the dominant near-term macro narrative. Markets are not yet pricing a full-scale escalation, but the tail risk is elevated.

- **Rising Real Yields Compressing Risk Assets:** The 10Y Treasury yield has risen for four consecutive sessions to 4.145%, with real yields (~1.77%) trending higher. Investors are dialling back Fed rate-cut expectations (now <50% odds of 2+ cuts in 2026 per CME data), driven by sticky inflation concerns amplified by the oil supply shock. This is the single most important headwind for equities (especially NQ) and gold.

- **Divergent Central Bank Paths Creating FX Opportunities:** The RBA is hiking (3.85%, March hike possible), the BoJ is on a gradual tightening path, and the ECB is holding steady — all while the Fed is expected to cut modestly. These divergences create selective FX opportunities: JPY and EUR have structural tailwinds from narrowing rate differentials, while AUD is caught between hawkish RBA and risk-off/China headwinds.
