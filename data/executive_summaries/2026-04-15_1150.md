## News Snapshot

**Futures:** Futures trade opened in a calmer tone after a strong risk rebound, with Reuters and other market coverage describing U.S. index futures as broadly steady while investors waited for earnings and assessed whether the Middle East relief rally had run its course. In energy, oil gave back part of its earlier war premium as diplomacy headlines improved, but the market remained supported by an only partially resolved supply-risk backdrop.

That left the broader FX picture supportive for non-USD contracts, especially where domestic policy remains comparatively firm, such as the Australian dollar, while the yen continued to benefit from a more constructive medium-term rate-differential narrative.

The cross-asset picture is constructive in selective areas rather than uniformly bullish.
