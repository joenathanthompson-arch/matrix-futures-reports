## Key Macro Themes

**Theme 1 — Iran War Energy Shock Driving Stagflation Risk:** The US-Iran conflict (escalated February 2026) has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz, driving Brent crude to ~$103-104/bbl (>50% above pre-war levels). This energy shock is simultaneously pushing inflation higher (ECB raised 2026 forecast to 2.6%, BoE expects inflation to hit 3.5%) while slowing growth (GDPNow 2.0%, Eurozone PMI near stall at 50.5). Central banks globally are in a "reluctant hold" or "hawkish hike" mode, unable to cut rates despite slowing growth. This stagflation dynamic is the dominant macro theme and explains the broad bearish bias across equities and precious metals.

**Theme 2 — Rising Real Yields Repricing Risk Assets:** The 10Y real yield has risen from ~1.80% in February to ~1.90-2.06% in March, driven by the energy-shock inflation repricing. This is the single most important factor for rate-sensitive assets (gold, tech, growth equities). The MOVE index's 52% YTD surge confirms that rate volatility is elevated and the bond market is still repricing. The 2s10s curve has flattened from 74 bps (January 30 peak) to 49 bps, signaling that the front end is rising faster than the long end — a classic "stagflation" yield curve pattern.

**Theme 3 — Central Bank Divergence Creating FX Opportunities:** The global central bank landscape is unusually divergent. The RBA hiked to 4.10% (hawkish), the BoJ is signaling hikes from 0.75% (hawkish turn), the ECB made a hawkish pivot (holding at 2.00% with hike bets rising), while the Fed holds at 3.50-3.75% in wait-and-see mode. This divergence is creating directional FX opportunities: JPY and EUR are benefiting from their central banks' hawkish turns, while AUD is caught between its own RBA hike and the risk-off environment.
