## Key Macro Themes

**Theme 1 — Falling Real Yields as the Dominant Structural Driver:** The 10-year TIPS yield (DFII10) has declined to 1.88%, down from ~2.0% in January 2026. This is the single most important macro factor across the scoring matrix, providing double-weighted bullish contributions to GC, ES, and NQ. The falling real yield environment reflects the market's pricing of the Fed's neutral hold stance combined with moderating inflation expectations, creating a favorable backdrop for gold and growth equities simultaneously.

**Theme 2 — Risk-Off Surge from Geopolitical Escalation:** The VIX spiked to 26.78 (+11.31%) and the MOVE index surged 28.24% to 108.84 on March 20, reflecting a significant risk-off episode driven by US military strikes and Iran tensions. WTI implied volatility reached 68% at its peak. This creates a bifurcated market: safe-haven assets (gold, JPY) benefit while risk-sensitive assets (AUD, small caps, credit) face headwinds. The geopolitical premium in crude oil is being offset by the fundamental inventory build.

**Theme 3 — Central Bank Divergence Driving FX Complexity:** Three central bank decisions in the past week (ECB hold at 2.0%, BoJ hold at 0.75%, RBA hike to 4.1%) create a complex FX landscape. The RBA's aggressive hiking cycle makes AUD fundamentally attractive on a rate basis, but the risk-off environment and China PMI contraction (49.0) overwhelm this positive. The ECB-Fed and BoJ-Fed differentials remain wide, keeping EUR and JPY under structural pressure despite safe-haven demand.
