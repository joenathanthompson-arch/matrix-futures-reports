## Dominant Macro Themes

The first theme is **broad USD softness**. The dollar index was lower on both a one-day and five-day basis, and that backdrop fed directly into constructive signals for gold, silver, the euro, the Australian dollar, and the yen.

The second theme is **growth deceleration rather than reacceleration in the United States**. The Atlanta Fed GDPNow estimate sat at **1.3% for 2026:Q1**, and the U.S. yield curve flattened further. In the model, that combination restrains the economically sensitive equity benchmarks and prevents crude from scoring more strongly despite geopolitical support.

The third theme is **physical oil-market disruption**. The IEA’s April 2026 Oil Market Report described a loss of more than **13 mb/d** of oil exports, severe restrictions through the Strait of Hormuz, and sharp cumulative supply losses in March and April. That is the main reason crude remains slightly bullish even after the latest EIA inventory build.

## Instrument Commentary

**GC and SI** both screen mildly constructive rather than strongly bullish. The positive impulse from dollar weakness, softer growth, and oil-driven inflation pressure is partially offset by a **hawkish Fed backdrop**, **flat real yields**, and the latest **gold ETF outflows**. Silver gains an extra industrial-demand tailwind from firm copper, which keeps it aligned with gold on signal even though the underlying mix is somewhat different.

**CL** stays modestly positive because geopolitical stress and the documented supply shock outweigh the bearish weekly U.S. inventory change. The crude score is not higher because the growth input is not yet supportive; if U.S. and global activity indicators weaken further, demand concerns could begin to dominate the supply story.

**ES and NQ** are neutral. Both benchmarks benefit from a weaker USD, and Nasdaq also receives support from a stronger semiconductor complex. Even so, the model does not award enough positive points to overcome the drag from softer growth, while flat real yields and a non-extreme VIX regime keep the overall signal restrained.

**YM and RTY** are the most vulnerable equity contracts in the framework. Their scoring places heavier emphasis on cyclical growth and curve shape, and both of those inputs turned unhelpful. With credit spreads broadly unchanged rather than tightening, there is no offsetting macro relief for either contract.

**M6E** is mildly bullish. A softer USD, firmer euro-area manufacturing PMI, and a still-hawkish ECB communication stance leave the euro modestly supported, although the report treats the near-term rate differential versus the dollar as effectively stable.

**6A** is the standout bullish market. The combination of a hawkish RBA, improved Australia-U.S. rate spread, recovering China manufacturing activity, and firmer copper prices generates a high-conviction signal. Because this score is catalyst-sensitive, it should be monitored closely for decay if China or commodity momentum fades.

**6J** is mildly bullish. The score reflects a softer dollar and a Bank of Japan stance that remains more supportive of yen strength than in prior cycles. However, the absence of a fully risk-off volatility regime keeps the signal from moving into a higher-conviction tier.

## Trading Implications

The cleanest tactical theme is to **favor AUD strength** and maintain a **defensive tilt within U.S. equities**. That means the methodology is more comfortable owning or expressing upside in **6A** than in broad U.S. index futures, while **YM** and **RTY** appear least attractive on a relative basis.

For commodities, the report supports maintaining a **measured bullish bias** rather than an aggressive chase. Gold, silver, and crude all carry positive scores, but none is yet supported by enough aligned factors to justify treating them as strong-conviction breakouts.
