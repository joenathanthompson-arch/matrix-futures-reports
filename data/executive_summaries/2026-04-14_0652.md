## Detailed Instrument Analysis

### GC — Gold

**Final Bias Score:** +2 (SLIGHT_BULLISH)

Gold retains only a slight bullish bias. The weaker dollar, slowing U.S. growth narrative, and tighter oil-supply backdrop support bullion, but those positives are offset by record March gold-ETF outflows and the lack of a fresh drop in real yields.

### SI — Silver

**Final Bias Score:** +1 (SLIGHT_BULLISH)

Silver also sits in slight bullish territory. USD weakness and the slower U.S. growth narrative help, but the precious-metals complex is capped by negative ETF-flow data and by the decision to keep copper flat until a fully readable live source is recovered.

### CL — Crude Oil

**Final Bias Score:** +5 (STRONG_BULLISH)

WTI crude is the clearest high-conviction long in this run. The official EIA outlook still centers on the Hormuz disruption, projected production shut-ins are large, geopolitical risk remains elevated, and a softer dollar adds support even after the latest inventory build.

### ES — S&P 500

**Final Bias Score:** +1 (SLIGHT_BULLISH)

The S&P 500 is only slight bullish rather than a stronger long. A softer dollar and slower-growth policy backdrop are supportive, but widening credit spreads and a flat VIX direction keep the signal moderate.

### NQ — Nasdaq 100

**Final Bias Score:** +2 (SLIGHT_BULLISH)

Nasdaq futures remain constructive but not high-conviction. SOX leadership is positive and the dollar remains weak, yet rising MOVE says rates volatility is no longer helping growth stocks the way it was in the prior report.

### YM — Dow Jones

**Final Bias Score:** +3 (BULLISH)

Dow futures are bullish because the steepening curve and softer dollar more than offset widening credit spreads. The score is stronger than ES because the Dow benefits more from the curve signal.

### RTY — Russell 2000

**Final Bias Score:** +2 (SLIGHT_BULLISH)

Russell 2000 remains slight bullish. The small-cap basket benefits from the weaker dollar, slowing-growth backdrop, and a steeper curve, but the widening in high-yield spreads prevents a full bullish upgrade.

### 6E — Euro

**Final Bias Score:** +1 (SLIGHT_BULLISH)

Euro futures are only slight bullish. The weaker dollar and mildly hawkish ECB backdrop help, but the latest ECB bulletin downgraded the euro-area growth outlook, which limits conviction.

### 6A — Australian Dollar

**Final Bias Score:** +5 (STRONG_BULLISH)

Australian dollar futures are strongly bullish. The AUD continues to benefit from the hawkish RBA backdrop, a supportive rate differential versus the Fed, a rebound in China PMI, and broad USD weakness. Copper was intentionally left neutral because live verification was incomplete, which keeps the score conservative.

### 6J — Japanese Yen

**Final Bias Score:** +3 (BULLISH)

Japanese yen futures stay bullish. The ongoing policy-divergence narrative versus a neutral Fed combines with dollar weakness to support JPY, even though the immediate BoJ meeting signal itself remains a hold.
