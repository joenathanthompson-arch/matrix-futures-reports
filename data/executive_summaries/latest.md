## Key Macro Themes

**1. Iran War / Strait of Hormuz Crisis:** The eruption of conflict in the Persian Gulf and near-closure of the Strait of Hormuz is the dominant macro event of the week. It has driven WTI crude +22%, VIX +48%, and created a complex cross-asset environment where traditional safe-haven relationships are being tested. Gold is underperforming despite the risk-off environment because rising real yields and USD strength are offsetting safe-haven demand.

**2. Real Yield / USD Regime Shift:** The simultaneous rise in real yields (+6.8bps WoW) and USD strength (+1.28% WoW) represents a "stagflationary" signal — the oil shock is pushing inflation expectations higher while growth expectations are falling. This combination is historically bearish for risk assets and gold (which needs falling real yields to outperform), and is the dominant factor in the current scoring.

**3. Central Bank Divergence (BoJ/ECB vs Fed):** While the Fed remains on hold with rate cuts expected later in 2026, the BoJ is on a tightening path and the ECB has shifted to a hawkish hold. This divergence is the primary driver of the FX bullish bias, particularly for JPY (+4) and EUR (+1).

---

4. **China PMI Contraction Limiting Commodity Upside**: China's official manufacturing PMI fell to 49.0 in February (4-month low), limiting the upside for AUD and industrial metals (copper). This creates a divergence within commodities — energy (oil) and precious metals (gold) are bid on geopolitical/safe haven demand, while industrial metals face China demand headwinds.
