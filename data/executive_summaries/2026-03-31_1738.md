## Key Macro Themes

1. **The Warsh Shock and Real Yield Dominance**: The market's repricing of U.S. monetary policy under the new sound-money doctrine has pushed real yields (DFII10) to 2.04%, creating structural headwinds for gold, equities, and any non-yielding asset. The $11B gold ETF exodus in March is the clearest expression of this regime change — institutions are rotating from gold into high-yielding cash and short-duration Treasuries.

2. **US-Iran War: Geopolitical Floor but Not a Catalyst**: The ongoing conflict in the Strait of Hormuz has elevated oil prices to ~$99/barrel and keeps geopolitical risk premium alive. However, the inventory build (+6.9Mb) signals that demand is not keeping pace with supply disruption fears, limiting oil's upside. The war's primary market impact is safe-haven JPY demand and elevated VIX, not a commodity supercycle.

3. **Central Bank Divergence Driving FX**: The BoJ hawkish pivot, ECB rate hike signals, and RBA's second hike of 2026 are all creating meaningful divergence from the Fed's neutral hold. This is the dominant bullish force for FX instruments against USD, with 6J as the highest-conviction expression of this theme.
