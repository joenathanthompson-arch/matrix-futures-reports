## Key Macro Themes

**Theme 1: The Geopolitical Inflation Trap.** US strikes on Iran have created a self-reinforcing feedback loop: higher oil prices (WTI $101.26) → higher inflation expectations → Fed hawkish hold (with 45% hike probability) → higher real rates → pressure on growth. This explains the paradox of a slowing GDPNow (1.6%) coexisting with tight credit spreads (3.16% HY OAS) — markets believe the Fed will not cut even as growth slows, creating a stagflationary risk premium.

**Theme 2: Non-USD Central Bank Divergence.** The ECB, RBA, and BoJ are all in hawkish mode relative to the Fed's hold, creating a structural USD headwind. EUR and JPY rate differentials are widening, and AUD's rate differential is also widening — but AUD is constrained by risk-off sentiment. This is the dominant FX theme for the next 4-6 weeks until the April 29 ECB and April 27 BoJ meetings.

**Theme 3: Credit Calm Supports Equities Despite VIX Elevation.** The disconnect between an elevated VIX (23.87, risk-off) and tightening credit spreads (HY OAS 3.16%, near multi-year tights) suggests that equity volatility is driven by geopolitical uncertainty rather than fundamental credit stress. When VIX is elevated but credit is calm, equities historically recover once the geopolitical catalyst resolves. This supports the bullish indices bias but warrants half-size positioning.
