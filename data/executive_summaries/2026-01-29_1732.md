## Key Macro Themes

The dominant narrative is **USD weakness driving broad asset class strength**. The DXY is down 2.2% over five days and 10.86% over one year, creating tailwinds for commodities, foreign currencies, and US equities. This weakness reflects market expectations of Fed dovishness relative to other central banks.

**Central bank divergence** is the second major theme. While the Fed remains on hold with neutral stance, both the RBA and BoJ are in tightening mode. This creates powerful rate differential dynamics, particularly for AUD and JPY. The ECB remains neutral but is concerned about Euro strength, limiting upside.

**Credit market health** continues to support risk assets. High-yield spreads have narrowed to 2.71%, indicating strong credit conditions. This is particularly bullish for small caps (RTY) and supports equity indices broadly. The combination of healthy credit and strong growth (GDPNow 5.4%) creates a constructive backdrop for risk-taking.
