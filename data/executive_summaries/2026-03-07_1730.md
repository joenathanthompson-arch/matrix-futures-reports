## Key Macro Themes

**1. US-Iran War Dominates Risk Sentiment.** The ongoing US-Iran military conflict is the single most important macro driver. The conflict has pushed WTI 1-month implied volatility to 51% (down from a peak of 68%), sustained a geopolitical risk premium in crude oil, and driven safe-haven flows into gold and JPY. Iranian missile and drone attacks on Arab allies of the US have broadened the conflict's scope. Markets are in a "wait and see" mode regarding Iran's response to recent US strikes. A ceasefire announcement would sharply reverse the GC, CL, and 6J bullish biases.

**2. Risk-Off Regime Confirmed by VIX and MOVE Surge.** The VIX's surge to 29.49 (+24% on March 6) and the MOVE index's rise to 81.26 (+9% on day, +41% on month) confirm a broad risk-off regime. The SPX skew is in the 99th percentile, indicating institutional hedging demand rather than panic selling — suggesting the risk-off move has further to run. This environment is supportive of gold and JPY but damaging for equities, commodity currencies (AUD), and credit-sensitive instruments (RTY).

**3. Central Bank Divergence Driving FX Opportunities.** The macro environment is creating meaningful FX opportunities through central bank divergence. The ECB's hawkish repricing (50% chance of 2026 hike) is bullish 6E. The BoJ's gradual tightening path versus the Fed's expected cuts (~37bp in 2026) is bullish 6J. The RBA's February hike and "every meeting is live" stance is bullish 6A in isolation, but risk-off overwhelms this signal. The Fed remains firmly on hold (96.3% no-change probability at March FOMC).
